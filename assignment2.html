The Olympics

The Olympic Games is an international sporting event featuring over 200 nations of the world. Since its inception in 393AD, the Olympics has been held once every 4 years. The Olympics comprises of the Summer and Winter games - which are held in alternative periods. Countries and Nations of Men and Women strive for Olympic success in the Summer, and in the Winter, in a competition of respect and sportsmanship.

This Visualisation explores the Olympic Games from 1992 to 2016, where 7 Summer Games and 6 Winter Games make up the time period. It explores participant effiencies, medal breakdown, gender distribution, and overall dominant summer and winter nations

Participant Efficiency

The Olympics casts away classifications of ethnicity. Those that compete are equal as humans. However, differing technology, training processes, mindsets, and levels of determination could set dominant olympic countries apart. The visualisation on the right is a projection of the efficiency of nations in the absolute number of athletes sent over the years, and the relative success of medals earnt from those atheletes.

Nations with a higher Participant Medal Rate translates to winning more medals per athlete sent to compete.

The most efficient country between 1992 and 2016 is the United States of America, with a Medal Won per 0.30 Participants.

Successful Olympic Nations

Success at the Olympics is determined by the Number of Medals Won. With the weighting of each Gold, Silver, and Bronze medal differing from year to year, the debate for the fairest system is still present.

Top Summer and Winter Olympic Medal Winning Countries

The Summer and Winter games are two drastically different environments. Countries dominant in one may not be as dominant in the other.

The Summer games consists of events such as athletics, archery, swimming, and gymnastics. The Summer games are seen to be the bigger of the two games as there are more medals to be won, and the nature of the sports allows many non-winter nations to participate and train for.

The Winter games consists of events such as speed skating, skiing, snowboarding, and bobsleighing. Due to the requirement of snow in order to train and build on skills and techniques, many humid - hot weathered nations are unable to provide athletes for the Winter games.

Between 1992 and 2016, the USA has heavily dominated in the Summer games. Their unmatched 7,000+ athletes with a medal winning rate of 0.30 sets them head and shoulders ahead of every other country in terms of medal count.

In the Winter games, Canada leads the way with number of medals won. Canada's available cold weather and mountain slopes allows for a perfect environment for Winter game atheletes to train. The lack of Asian, South American, African, and Oceanic success in the Winter games highlights the importance of such environments for its athletes to succeed.
